Metallica, an influential American heavy metal band that, along with Slayer and Anthrax, developed
the speed metal subgenre in the early to mid-1980s. The core members were vocalist and rhythm guitarist
James Hetfield (born August 3, 1963, in Downey, California, USA), drummer Lars
Ulrich (born December 26, 1963, in Gentofte, Denmark), lead guitarist Kirk Hammett
(born November 18, 1962, in San Francisco, California), and bassist Cliff Burton
(born February 10, 1962, in San Francisco—died September 27, 1986,
near Stockholm, Sweden). Jason Newsted (born March 4, 1963, in Battle Creek, Michigan) took over on bass
after Burton died in a bus accident while on tour.
